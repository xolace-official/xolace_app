We're building for Ghana first, but this problem doesn't stop at any border.

100 down. A long, necessary road ahead.

## Post 3 — Big Players Haven't Come Here
**Type:** Thought leadership / cultural insight
**Account:** Personal (founder)
**Timing:** 2–3 days after Post 2 (the two work as a sequence — Post 2 is the proof, Post 3 is the context)

### LinkedIn

Pick any top mental health app. Calm. Headspace. BetterHelp.

Ask 10 people in Ghana if they've used one. You'll be lucky to find 1 who's even heard of it.

I keep coming back to two possible explanations, and I'm honestly not sure which one sits worse with me.

The first is that the market just looked too hard — infrastructure gaps, payment friction, weak purchasing power signals. A strategic wait that quietly became a permanent backburner.

The second is on us, and it's harder to say out loud.

Ours is a culture built on resilience. You carry what you carry and you keep moving. Talking about what's going on inside you — not to solve a problem, just to name it — isn't something most of us were raised to see as useful. It can feel self-indulgent, even weak.

Maybe they looked at that and saw no demand worth chasing.

But a culture that doesn't talk about emotional struggle isn't a culture without it. The people who grew up being told to push through aren't less affected. They're just less visible. And that invisibility got read as absence.

I don't know if the big players made a deliberate call or just never looked closely enough. Either way, something real got left on the table.

---

### Twitter/X

Ask 10 people in Ghana if they've used Calm, Headspace, or BetterHelp.

9 will say no. The 1 probably just caught the name somewhere.

These platforms have been around for years. They have the resources and the reach. So why hasn't this part of the world been a priority?

Could be the infrastructure. The payment friction. Markets that don't look profitable on paper.

Or it could be that they looked at a culture where carrying things silently is just what you do — where naming what you feel was never really taught as something worth doing — and read that as no real demand.

Those are two very different problems. One is logistics. The other is a misread of what silence actually means.

---

### Instagram

The biggest mental health apps in the world have been around for years.

Most people in Ghana have never heard of them.

Some of that is infrastructure. Some of it is markets that got deprioritised before anyone looked too closely.

But some of it — the part I keep thinking about — is that we come from a place where endurance was the answer to everything. You carry it, you keep moving, you don't stop to examine what you're feeling because no one ever told you that was a worthwhile thing to do.

So from the outside, the demand looked like it wasn't there.

It was there. It just had nowhere to go.
