## Problem Statement

Xolace has a core privacy promise: we do not collect email or personal data. This is a trust contract with every user. But YC demands founder obsession with users — real conversations, not survey data. The challenge: how do you talk to your users when you've promised not to hold their contact info?

The problem breaks into two distinct research needs:
1. **Current user research**: Why do people stop using Xolace? Does the mirror actually help?
2. **Future/non-user research**: What stops people from downloading? What does the target user do instead?

## Demand Evidence

No direct evidence that existing app users want to be contacted by Xolace. This is important: the absence of a request to be contacted is not evidence that users would reject it — but it means any contact channel must be purely opt-in. Users who seek out a feedback channel are higher-signal than users who respond to a survey prompt.

Prior session evidence: 5 users gave verbatim quotes that generated outreach DMs (May 1 assignment). The existence of verbatim quotes suggests some users are already expressing strong feelings — a signal that the right opt-in channel would find willing participants.

## Target User (Named)

There is a specific nameable person who represents the user most worth understanding right now. The research goals are:
- Understanding what triggers them to open Xolace vs. sitting with the feeling alone
- Understanding what makes them stop coming back
- Understanding whether the mirror reflection made them feel genuinely heard or just acknowledged

## Constraints

- Privacy-first architecture: no email, no personal data stored in Xolace's backend
- Any contact mechanism must be user-initiated and opt-in
- Any data exchanged during research conversations lives outside Xolace's systems (scheduling tools, call recordings)
- Team resources: Nathan (engineering), Emmanuel (community outreach, can run interviews), Somuah (backend/AI)

## Premises (Confirmed)

1. The privacy contract prevents active outreach to existing users (no cold contact)
2. The privacy contract does NOT prevent building a passive opt-in channel that users discover and choose to use
3. For PMF research, non-users who match the target profile are as valuable as current users
4. Depth beats scale: 5 real 30-minute conversations >> 500 survey responses for churn/mirror questions
5. Emmanuel + university network is an untapped, unconstrained asset for non-user research

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: In-App Opt-In Link
Single discoverable link in settings or session-end screen: "Want to help shape Xolace? We'd love to hear your story." Tapping opens an external scheduling tool (Cal.com, Typeform). Xolace's backend never touches the contact info. User-initiated, user-controlled.

Effort: S (1-2 hours engineering). Risk: Low.
Pros: Privacy-respecting, self-selects high-signal users, reaches current users.
Cons: Low discovery rate (~2-5%), misses churned users.

### Approach B: Guerrilla Non-User Research via Emmanuel + University Network
No app changes. Emmanuel runs 10 structured 20-minute observation sessions this week with people from the university network who match the Xolace profile (18-30, emotionally aware, not in therapy). Script: show the app, watch them use it, don't help. Separately, post in university WhatsApp/Discord groups asking for 10-minute testers.

Effort: S (zero engineering, ~5 hours Emmanuel's time). Risk: Low.
Pros: Zero privacy tension, observation-based, reaches non-users who can say why they wouldn't download.
Cons: Doesn't reach existing users who churned; sample may be younger/more educated than actual user base.

### Approach C: "Campfire Circle" — Permanent Opt-In Community
A Telegram or Discord labeled "Xolace: The quiet room" — private, invite-only, mentioned in App Store description and settings. Not a social network; a structured research space.

Effort: M (2 hours setup + Emmanuel 1hr/week ongoing). Risk: Medium (brand tension with "private sanctuary" identity).

## Recommended Approach

**A + B in parallel.** Add the opt-in link to the session-end screen (Approach A, ~1 hour) AND have Emmanuel run 10 non-user conversations this week (Approach B, 0 engineering).

Within 7 days you'll have:
- Signal from current users who care enough to opt in (A)
- Signal from non-users who've never used the app (B)

Both with zero privacy violation and zero new infrastructure.

**Defer Approach C** until A + B are exhausted. Community infrastructure requires sustained investment; the brand tension is real. Build it only if you find you have more willing participants than you can talk to.

## Interview Script Skeleton

**Before every call:** Disclose recording. One sentence: "I'd like to record this for my notes — is that okay?" If no, don't record; take written notes instead. This is not optional.

For Approach A (current users, via call/video):

*If user is active (used app in last 2 weeks):*
1. "Tell me about the last time you opened Xolace. What was happening?"
2. "What did the reflection say back to you? How did that land?"
3. "What do you do on days when you don't open Xolace? What does that look like?"
4. "If Xolace disappeared tomorrow, would you notice? What would you miss?"

*If user is churned (hasn't opened in 3+ weeks):*
1. "Tell me about the last time you remember using Xolace. What was the moment?"
2. "What was your experience of it — did it feel like it got what you were going through?"
3. "What made you stop coming back? Was there a specific moment, or just drift?"
4. "Is there something it should have done that it didn't?"

Note: A "surprise" counts when the user says or does something that contradicts what you assumed. Examples: they use it at a specific time you didn't expect, they skipped a step you thought was central, they described the mirror in a way that differs from your intent. Write it verbatim.

For Approach B (non-users, observation-based):
1. "I'm going to show you something. Just use it and tell me what you're thinking out loud."
2. (Watch silently. Note: what do they hesitate on? What do they skip? What surprises them?)
3. After: "What did the app think you were feeling? Did it get it right?"
4. "Is there anything it didn't say that would have been more accurate?"

## Placement Decision (Resolved)

The opt-in link goes on the **session-end activity variant** ("You showed up for yourself today" screen) only — not the exit variant ("Heard." screen). Rationale: the activity variant is reached after the user completed a path (solo/peers), meaning they engaged meaningfully with the product. The exit variant is a minimal close and adding a request there feels extractive. The activity variant has a natural pause and a positive emotional note; a quiet "Want to help shape Xolace? [Talk to us]" fits without feeling intrusive.

**Not in settings**: Settings is a low-traffic screen and most users who visit it are looking to change something, not start a conversation.

**Contact mechanism — dual link (resolved)**: Two quiet links below "Have more? I'm here." on the activity variant. Neither touches Xolace's backend.

1. **"Book a call"** → Cal.com booking page (`https://cal.com/nathan/xolace-conversation` — create free-tier page first per Assignment). Committed participants: user picks a slot, both parties are bound. Name+email stay in Cal.com only.
2. **"Write to us"** → `mailto:nathan@xolaceinc.com` with subject pre-filled. Zero setup, lower barrier. Softer commitment — scheduling happens via email reply. Useful for users who want to reach out but aren't ready to commit to a call time.

Rationale: a commitment ladder. "Book a call" filters for high-signal participants; "Write to us" captures users who are willing but hesitant. Both are passive opt-in; user taps voluntarily.

**Who runs the calls**: Nathan runs the first 10-20. YC explicitly says founders should do early user interviews themselves — not delegate to COO or PM. The insights that matter most are the ones that land on the person making product decisions.

## Fallback

If the opt-in link yields 0 bookings in 3 weeks: the discovery rate assumption (2-5%) was too optimistic, or the link placement is wrong. Escalation steps in order:
1. Move the link to a more prominent position (above the fold on session-end, or add a single in-app prompt once per user, 30 days after first session)
2. Ask the 5 verbatim-quote users from the May 1 assignment directly — they already expressed strong feelings, which is implicit consent to engage further
3. Proceed with Approach B only until Approach A is revisited
