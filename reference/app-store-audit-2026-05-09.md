  Subtitle — 4/10

  Current: Understand what you feel (24/30 chars)

  The copy is emotionally resonant and on-brand. The problem is it contains zero high-volume search terms. Users searching for this category
  type things like: anxiety relief, mood tracker, emotional journal, mental wellness, feelings journal, self reflection. None of those words
  appear here.

  The 6 unused characters are also a minor miss.

  Keyword Field — Not Auditable

  The 100-character keyword field isn't visible externally. This needs a review inside App Store Connect. Critical things to audit there:
  - No repetition of "Xolace", "feel", or "understand" (already in title/subtitle)
  - No spaces after commas
  - Singular forms only (Apple indexes plurals automatically)
  - Are all 100 characters used?

  Description — 5/10

  Strengths:
  - Clear privacy differentiation ("no ads, no data sales")
  - Honest scope-setting (not therapy, not a companion)
  - The campfire/mirror metaphor is distinctive

  Weaknesses:
  - The first three lines (above the fold without tapping "more") are critical, and the current lead buries the value proposition in abstract
  framing
  - The "what it's NOT" list is intellectually honest but leads with negatives — users scanning need a positive hook first
  - No social proof (press mentions, user count, notable quotes)
  - On iOS, description carries only 5% weight for ranking — but it's where conversion happens after a user clicks in

  ---
  Preview Video — 0/10

  No preview video exists. This is a significant miss. Preview videos autoplay in search results and category browse — they're one of the
  highest-conversion surface areas Apple offers. A 20-second video showing the "What's here right now?" → mirror → path selection flow would
  dramatically improve click-to-install rate.

  - The "not enough ratings" display actively signals low credibility to potential users
  - Competitor Wysa has 24K ratings; Reflectly has 82K

  The stars themselves are perfect — the issue is pure volume.

  Quick Wins — Do These Today

  1. Rewrite the title to include a keyword
  Proposal: Xolace: Emotional Journal & AI (34 chars → trim to 30)
  Or: Xolace – Mood & Feelings Journal (33 chars → trim)
  Or: Xolace: AI Emotional Reflection (31 chars → trim to 30)
  The brand is protected; the colon buys you keyword space. Pick whichever category keyword has the highest volume in your App Store Connect
  search tab.

  2. Rewrite the subtitle to hit search terms
  Current: Understand what you feel
  Proposal: Mood journal & emotional clarity (32 chars → trim to 30)
  Or: AI journal for anxiety & stress (31 → trim to 30)
  Keep the emotional honesty of the brand but speak the user's search language.

  3. Set the Promotional Text field
  This 170-char field sits above the description, changes without App Review, and is the first text most users read. Use it for a timely hook:
  "A quiet space to name what you're carrying — no therapist, no judgment, just clarity."

  4. Audit and max out the keyword field
  Open App Store Connect → App → iOS App → Keywords. Ensure you're using all 100 characters with no repetition from title/subtitle, no spaces
  after commas, relevant terms like:
  emotional,wellness,mood,journal,anxiety,feelings,mental,reflection,self,care,mindfulness,calm,stress,therapy.

  5. Add a What's New entry on next update
  Even minor updates give you a chance to surface keywords in the What's New field and signal active development to the algorithm.

  High-Impact Changes — This Week

  1. Launch a rating prompt campaign
  Instrument expo-store-review (or react-native-rate) to trigger the native iOS rating dialog at the right moment — after a user completes
  their first full session (Mirror confirmed → Path selected → Session end). The natural emotional completion of a session is exactly the
  moment users feel heard and are most likely to rate positively. Getting from 2 → 50+ ratings is the single highest-leverage action available.

  2. Film a 20-second App Preview video
  Use the iOS Simulator or a physical device. Show: app opening → "What's here right now?" prompt → user typing → AI mirror appearing → path
  selection. No voiceover needed — overlay text cards. Upload to App Store Connect under App Previews. This autoplays in search results.

  3. Fill all 10 screenshot slots
  If you're not using all 10, you're leaving a third of your visual real estate empty. Screenshots 1–3 are the most critical — they appear
  before "Show All" in search results. Each should carry a text overlay with a concrete benefit, not a feature description.

  4. Add screenshots for 6.7" (iPhone 15 Pro Max)
  Apple serves the device-appropriate screenshots. Missing the 6.7" set means iPhone 15 Pro Max users (a large and growing cohort) may see
  scaled screenshots.

  Strategic Recommendations — This Month

  1. Apply for Apple's Mental Wellness editorial featuring
  The App Store editorial team actively curates mental health apps, especially privacy-first ones. Xolace's privacy architecture is a
  legitimate differentiator. Submit an app story request through App Store Connect → App → App Store Features → Product Page. This is free and
  can drive significant organic installs.

  2. Create a second product page targeting "anxiety journal"
  Apple's Custom Product Pages let you create up to 35 alternate pages with different screenshots, promotional text, and preview video — all
  indexed separately. A page optimized for "anxiety journal" searchers with screenshots showing the stress/anxiety use case would capture a
  distinct high-volume keyword cluster without changing your default listing.

  3. Localize for UK and Australia English
  These are large English-speaking markets with high mental health app adoption. Even a light localization pass (spelling adjustments + a
  localized subtitle) unlocks separate ranking opportunities in those storefronts at essentially zero marginal cost.

  4. Monitor keyword rankings weekly
  Set up free rank tracking (AppFollow free tier, or MobileAction) for 20-30 target keywords: emotional journal, mood tracker, feelings
  journal, mental wellness app, anxiety relief, self reflection, mindfulness journal. Without baseline data you're flying blind on whether any
  metadata changes work.

  5. Build toward 500 ratings before any major push
  Any paid UA, PR, or Product Hunt launch before you have a credible rating count will underperform. The App Store's social proof threshold is
  roughly 100+ ratings before conversion rates normalize. Plan metadata + rating velocity work as the prerequisite to any distribution play.

  ---
  The core story here is that Xolace has a genuinely differentiated product with a 5-star rating buried under a brand-only title and near-zero
  social proof. The ranking and discovery machinery isn't engaged yet. Fixing the title and starting a rating prompt flow are the two changes
  that will move the needle fastest.
