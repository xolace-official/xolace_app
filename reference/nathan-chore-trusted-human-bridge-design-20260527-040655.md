# Design: The Trusted Bridge

Generated by /office-hours on 2026-05-27

Mode: Startup

## Problem Statement

Users come to Xolace because they cannot find the words for what they are feeling. The Mirror solves
that — it gives them precise language for themselves. But once they have the words, a natural second
want appears: to tell someone in their life. And the same barrier reappears. "I don't know how to put
this." The articulation problem recurs at the interpersonal hop.

The Trusted Bridge is the second half of the existing flow. Not a feature add. Not growth infrastructure.
A completion mechanism for sessions where the user doesn't just want to be heard by the app — they want
to be heard by a specific person.

## Demand Evidence

No formal research yet. What exists:
- Multiple users DMing the founder with "I don't know how to put this" — wanting to share what they
  processed but stuck at the interpersonal translation step.
- Users already improvising: the native share sheet integration (react-native-share, PR #73) was built
  for quotes, but the behavior it enables (sharing emotional content from Xolace to messaging apps) is
  exactly the pattern the Bridge formalizes.
- Status quo cost: the feeling sits with them and the moment passes. The send never happens.

Validation still needed: talk to 5 users who have completed 5+ sessions. Ask "Have you ever wanted to
tell someone what you were feeling after a session? What stopped you?" This should happen before the
full AI drafting build.

## Status Quo

Users who want to share after a session have no structured path. They:
1. Try to compose a message from scratch in iMessage or WhatsApp — hit the blank page, give up
2. Say something vague out loud later ("I've been a bit off lately") — diluted, under-articulated
3. Share the Mirror text directly — but it reads as an internal reflection, not a message to a person

The Mirror uses language like "a weight that feels like helplessness mixed with something unfinished" —
precise, but not how you text your mom. Translation is required, and users can't do it alone.

## Target User

The specific human: someone who has just had a real session (not a one-tap texture selection). They've
confirmed the Mirror. They felt heard. Now they want to tell someone — a parent, partner, close friend —
what they've been carrying. They know WHO to tell. They don't know HOW to start the message.

This is not every session. It's not even most sessions. It's the sessions where the user's first
instinct after "That's it" is to reach for their phone to text someone.

## Constraints

- Zero contact data to server: recipient context (name, relationship) is used in the Anthropic prompt
  only, never persisted to Convex
- Never auto-send: user must explicitly trigger the native share sheet and send from their messaging app
- Mirror ≠ Bridge message: the Mirror is internal language; the Bridge needs a separate translation draft
- Session-end screen is already crowded: `SessionEndNotifNudge`, `ReachFeedbackCard`, and the incoming
  referral card (from referral mechanism design) all compete for space. The Bridge must be optional and
  deferrable, not mandatory UI
- No contact picker for MVP: system contacts require additional privacy surface area and native permissions

## Premises

1. Mirror text is NOT shareable as-is — internal language, written for the user's clarity, not a
   recipient who lacks context. Defended against cross-model challenge with specific reasoning.
2. Articulation barrier reappears at the share step — same blankness, second hop, even with Mirror in hand.
3. Never-auto-send is non-negotiable — one wrong send in a vulnerable moment is product-destroying.
4. Contact data must never leave the device — Xolace's privacy contract is load-bearing.

## Cross-Model Perspective

Independent Claude subagent cold read:

> "The app completed its job (user now understands their feeling) and then abandoned them at the exact
> moment of highest motivation to act. The session ends and the door closes. The Trusted Bridge is
> actually the second half of the existing flow, not an add-on."

Key insight: the moat is NOT AI drafting (WhatsApp shipped that in March 2026). The moat is that
Xolace's draft is grounded in real emotional work the user just completed — not a blank prompt.

Challenge surfaced: Premise 1 ("Mirror not shareable as-is") was flagged as untested. Held after
pushback: the Mirror's introspective register is demonstrably different from interpersonal texting.
Validated by user experience of receiving Mirror-like language as a message — it reads as a therapist's
note, not a friend's text.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Template-Guided (Minimal)
Pre-written human template filled with `emotional_metadata` fields. Zero new Anthropic calls. Ships 2-3
days. Formulaic — might feel hollow for a feature in this emotional register.

Effort: S | Risk: Low | Completeness: 6/10

### Approach B: Full AI Draft (Selected)
Two screens: recipient context (free text) → AI-generated personalized draft → edit → native share.
New Convex action calls Anthropic with Mirror summary + recipient context. Contact data prompt-only, never
stored. Clean architecture, complete value proposition.

Effort: M | Risk: Med | Completeness: 9/10

### Approach C: Save-First / Draft Later
Same drafting as B, but default is "save to timeline" not "send." Decouples the vulnerable moment from
the send action. Creates "unsent letters" pattern with standalone therapeutic value. Higher emotional
safety, lower immediate completion rate.

Effort: M | Risk: Med | Completeness: 8/10

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B** with a lightweight validation gate before the full AI call build.

Ship in two stages:
1. **Stage 1 (validation):** Two screens — "Who is this for?" free text + Mirror text pre-filled in
   editable TextArea + share button. Zero AI call. Measures: how many users tap into the Bridge, how
   much they edit the Mirror text, do they actually share. This runs the flow with real users at minimal
   engineering cost. If <5% of eligible sessions trigger it, reconsider scope.
2. **Stage 2 (full):** Replace Mirror pre-fill with AI-generated draft. Same screen structure. Now the
   draft is written for the recipient, not copied from the Mirror.

The two-stage approach validates demand before committing to the AI cost.

### Flow Specification

**Entry point:** Session-end screen (`session-end.tsx`), both exit and activity variants (Stage 1
includes exit-variant sessions — simpler instrumentation; Stage 2 may restrict to Mirror-confirmed +
activity-completed only based on Stage 1 data). Bridge card renders inline in both `ExitVariant` and
`ActivityVariant` JSX — not in a queue mechanism. Existing `SessionEndNotifNudge` and `ReachFeedbackCard`
remain; queue coordination is a separate ticket. Text on card: "Find the words to tell someone."

**Screen 1 — Recipient Context** (`(protected)/trusted-bridge`)
- Header: "Who would you like to tell?"
- Free text field: name (e.g., "Mom", "Alex") — placeholder: "their name or how you think of them"
- Optional relationship selector (4 options: parent, partner, friend, sibling) — informs tone
- CTA: "Write this together"
- gestureEnabled: false on Screen 1 to prevent accidental back-swipe discarding recipient context mid-flow

**Screen 2 — Draft Review**
- Header: "Here's a start..." (not "Here's what to send" — leaves room for them)
- Full-width editable TextArea pre-filled with AI draft (Stage 2) or Mirror text (Stage 1)
- Secondary button: "Save without sending" — Stage 1: dismisses without any persistence (no Convex write,
  no timeline entry). On tap: `router.replace('/(protected)')` (home). The edit step itself is the
  "pause before sending." Stage 2 can revisit whether saving makes sense once demand is validated.
- Primary CTA: "Share..." → native share sheet (react-native-share, already integrated)
- Error state (Stage 2 only): if AI call fails, fall back to Mirror text pre-fill with footer note:
  "Couldn't personalize this — here's a starting point." Same UX as Stage 1; no error screen shown.
- Footer: tiny text — "This only lives on your device. We don't see who you're writing to."

**AI Prompt Design (Stage 2):**
```
You're helping someone write a message to {{recipientName}}, {{their relationship}}.

They just spent time reflecting and came to understand: {{mirrorText}}

Write a short message they could send to {{recipientName}} right now.

Rules:
- First person ("I've been...", "I wanted to tell you...")
- Natural texting tone — warm, not clinical
- 3-5 sentences max
- Don't mention apps, reflection, or Xolace
- Don't start with "I" (feels abrupt)
- This is a starting point, not a finished message — leave some space
- Match the emotional register: if the feeling is heavy, don't make it light
```

**What is NOT persisted:**
- recipientName
- recipientRelationship
- The generated draft text (ephemeral in-app state only)

**What IS included in analytics (PostHog client-side captures only — no new Convex fields for Stage 1):**
- `bridge_opened` event — fires when user taps the Bridge card
- `bridge_shared` event — fires when native share sheet is triggered; includes `{ character_delta: number, first_sentence_changed: boolean, recipient_relationship: string | null }` (no message content, no recipient name). `edit_count` dropped — no consistent counting definition.
- No `bridge_saved` event in Stage 1 (save button dismisses without persistence)

### Convex Architecture

New internal action: `convex/ai/bridge.ts`
```typescript
export const generateBridgeDraft = internalAction({
  args: {
    sessionId: v.id("sessions"),
    recipientName: v.string(),
    recipientRelationship: v.optional(v.union(
      v.literal("parent"),
      v.literal("partner"),
      v.literal("friend"),
      v.literal("sibling"),
    )),
  },
  handler: async (ctx, args) => {
    // 1. Load mirrorText from sessions table via ctx.runQuery(internal.sessions.getMirrorText, { sessionId })
    // 2. Build prompt from recipientName + mirrorText + relationship
    // 3. Single (non-streaming) Anthropic call — draft is 3-5 sentences, streaming adds complexity for no gain
    // 4. Return draft string — never store it
    return { draft: "<string>" };
  },
});
```

Use `sessions.mirrorText` (the confirmed Mirror text), NOT `distilledText`. `distilledText` is
populated asynchronously by `reflectionDistiller.ts` — not guaranteed available at session-end.
`mirrorText` is set synchronously when the Mirror is confirmed and is always present.
Route file `src/app/(protected)/trusted-bridge.tsx` is a thin wrapper; screen UI goes in
`src/features/trusted-bridge/components/screen/` following the existing pattern.

### Session-End Screen Crowding

Current session-end has: `SessionEndNotifNudge`, `ReachFeedbackCard` (after N sessions),
and the incoming referral card (referral mechanism design, chore(🫩)referral-mechanism).

Stage 1 rule: Bridge card renders inline in both variants, existing cards remain. No queue mechanism.
A coordinated card-queue with priority ordering is a separate ticket — do not block Stage 1 on it.
Track `bridge_opened` rate; if it's crowded out by existing cards, that's the signal to build the queue.

## Open Questions

1. **Entry point scope**: Resolved for Stage 1 — include exit-variant sessions (simpler, more data).
   Revisit restriction to Mirror-confirmed + activity-completed after Stage 1 data.
2. **Save-without-sending architecture**: Resolved for Stage 1 — save button dismisses without persistence.
   If Stage 1 shows demand, add persistence in Stage 2 as a separate spec.
3. **Second thoughts timing**: Out of scope for Stage 1. The edit step is the pause. Explicit timer
   deferred to Stage 2 if data shows impulsive shares being regretted.
4. **Session-end card queue**: Deferred — separate ticket. Stage 1 ships Bridge inline; queue coordination
   later.
5. **Tone validation**: Does the "relationship type" selector improve draft quality enough to justify
   the friction? A/B test with and without it in Stage 2.

## Success Criteria

Stage 1 (validation):
- Bridge card tap rate >8% of eligible sessions (Mirror confirmed, activity completed or exit)
- Share completion rate (triggered share sheet) >40% of Bridge opens
- Edit rate >60% — defined as: (Bridge opens where character delta > 0) / (Bridge opens). Shows engagement, not passive forwarding.

Stage 2 (full draft):
- Edit rate drops vs Stage 1 (AI draft requires less editing than Mirror text — validates the translation)
- Share completion rate increases vs Stage 1 (better draft quality reduces abandonment)

## Distribution Plan

In-app feature. No new distribution surface. Existing iOS/Android builds via EAS.
Leverages existing react-native-share integration (already shipping).
Native share sheet handles WhatsApp, iMessage, SMS, and all other platforms — no platform-specific work.

## Dependencies

- `sessions.mirrorText` available at session-end: confirmed, set synchronously at Mirror confirmation
- `react-native-share` integration: merged PR #73
- Anthropic client in Convex: already used in `convex/ai/process.ts`
- Referral card design (chore(🫩)referral-mechanism): will need card queue coordination at session end
- Session ownership validation (`requireSessionOwnership()`): already exists in `convex/lib/auth.ts`

## The Assignment

Before building Stage 2 (full AI draft), validate Stage 1 with real users.

Ship the 2-screen flow with Mirror pre-fill (no AI call). Let it run for 2 weeks. Look at:
- What % of sessions trigger the Bridge card?
- Of those who open it, how many tap Share?
- What do users edit? (Can't log content — but can log edit count, character delta)

If the edit rate is high (>60%) and share rate is solid (>40%), the demand is real and Stage 2 is
justified. If both are low, the feature needs a different entry point or framing before you invest
in the AI drafting infrastructure.

The assignment is: ship Stage 1 before writing a single line of Stage 2 Anthropic code.
