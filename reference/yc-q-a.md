# YC Interview Q&A

---

**What are you working on?**
Xolace is an app for when you feel something heavy but can't name what it is. You write it out, our AI tells you exactly what you're feeling, and then guides you through it. One session, done.

---

**What's your progress so far?**
We are live on both iOS and Android with 84 users and 224 sessions completed. We are averaging 20–22 users a week organically with zero marketing spend, and a week-1 retention average of 22% across 3 cohorts.

---

**What is your unique insight?**
Every AI mental health startup is betting that AI can replace the human layer — be your therapist, your friend, your support. We think that's the wrong bet. As AI becomes more familiar and common, human connection becomes more valuable, not less. What we discovered talking to users is that people are only turning to AI companions because the human layer is broken — not because they prefer a simulated relationship. So we go the unusual direction: we use AI to fix the human layer, not replace it. AI does the hard work of articulation and matching. Humans do the connecting.

---

**Is it good? (The solution)**
Most of our competitors fully bet on AI to replace the human layer. Others are just passive tools for meditation and sleep. Therapy apps require the user to already know they want therapy. We go in an unusual direction — leveraging AI to make the human layer work, not replace it.

---

**Who are your main competitors and why are you different?**
AI companion apps like Ash and Woebot focus on ongoing conversation to reach articulation — we do that in one shot and much faster. Therapy apps skip the articulation layer entirely. We start from the very top.

---

**Are you the team to do it?**
All of us have lived through what we're building for. We know this feeling personally. We've already shipped a working product with real users returning without any marketing. And for clinical depth, we're already in conversations with mental health professionals who want to advise us.

---

**How will you get your first 100 users?**
A third through partnerships with NGOs, non-profits, influencers, and communities. A third through Google and Apple paid ads with search-based intent. A third from word of mouth and a referral system.

## Compliance & Regulation (Research Later)

Areas to get familiar with before we scale — we don't need to be experts now but need to know what's coming:

- **HIPAA (US)** — If we ever store or process health data in a clinical context, this applies. Need to understand whether what we collect counts as PHI (protected health information).
- **GDPR (EU)** — If we have European users, their emotional/session data is sensitive personal data. Stricter consent and deletion rules.
- **FDA Digital Health Guidelines** — FDA has a framework for digital health tools. "General wellness" apps are low risk but once you make clinical-adjacent claims, scrutiny increases.
- **Apple & Google Mental Health App Policies** — Both stores have specific rules around mental health apps, crisis resources, and what you can claim.
- **Crisis & Escalation Duty of Care** — In some jurisdictions there are legal expectations around what a mental health product must do when a user signals they are in danger. Our escalation feature touches this.
- **COPPA (US)** — If any users are under 13, strict rules apply. Need a clear age gate.
- **State-level regulations (US)** — Some US states (e.g. California with CCPA) have their own data privacy laws beyond federal ones.

> Action: Research each of these more deeply before Series A or any clinical partnership conversations.




### What YC Might Ask About Compliance

**"Are you a HIPAA covered entity?"**
Not yet. We don't store clinical health records, we don't work with doctors or insurers. We store emotional expressions, not diagnoses. But we're watching it as we scale.

**"What happens if a user says they want to hurt themselves?"**
We detect crisis signals in real time. When triggered, we surface emergency resources immediately and don't let the session continue as normal. We don't try to be a crisis line — we route to ones that are.

**"How do you handle user data privacy?"**
Sessions are anonymized. We don't sell data. Users can delete everything. We built privacy-first from day one — it's core to why people trust us with something this personal.

**"Are you a medical device?"**
No. We're a wellness tool, not a diagnostic or treatment tool. We make no clinical claims. Same category as a meditation app.

**The posture to hold across all of these:**
"We know the landscape. We're not in clinical territory yet, but we know exactly which line we'd be crossing and we're intentionally staying on the right side of it."

---

**How big can you get?**
If we win, emotional processing becomes as normal as brushing your teeth. Right now people either perform wellness on social media or wait until things get bad enough for therapy. Therapy systems are overwhelmed — waitlists are months long. We become the daily layer underneath all of that. Not replacing therapists, but making sure fewer people reach crisis because they had somewhere to process early.
