## Problem Statement

Xolace's core thesis — AI mirrors help people name what they're feeling — is validated by users who say the mirror "hit the spot." But a parallel user need has emerged from direct feedback: users who just need to release, not name. Three out of three observed users reported burning or destroying what they wrote. They're not seeking articulation. They're seeking **erasure with witness** — a space to be seen by no one, that disappears immediately.

Simultaneously, peer reflections (anonymous text) provide intellectual recognition ("someone else felt this") but lack the embodied recognition that a real face and voice carries. No existing feature replicates "a human saying: I felt what you just described. I'm still here."

These two gaps define the design problem: add (1) ephemeral voice release and (2) human-scale recognition — without compromising the "not a chatbot, not social" design integrity.

---

## Demand Evidence

- User quote: "I just write a whole lot of nothing on a sheet of paper... trash it or burn it. Then I'm fine."
- User quote: "I sleep it off. Only when it's too much."
- Observed behavior: chatbot expectation on first use (users expected to converse, not be mirrored)
- Observed reaction: "surprised at how the mirror actually hit the spot" — surprise implies low expectation, high fulfillment
- Market signal: every voice mental health app in 2025-2026 (Headspace Ebb, Yuna, Kintsugi Voice) builds conversational AI that stores memory. No one has built ephemeral voice-as-release deliberately.

---

## Status Quo

The burn-the-paper users have a ritual. It works for them on bad days. What it doesn't give them: language. The Xolace mirror gives them language. But some days, they don't want language — they want to scream into something that disappears. Currently, there is no digital equivalent of burning paper. There are voice memos (stores everything), voice journaling apps (stores, analyzes), and AI chat (stores, responds). The ephemeral vent box is an unclaimed space.

---

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Mirror flow user:** Needs to understand what they're feeling. 25-35, uses journaling apps, tried therapy or thought about it. "What is this feeling?" is their actual question.

**Vent box user:** Doesn't want to name it. Just needs it out. The burn-the-paper person. Finds journaling apps unsatisfying because the words stay.

**Glimpses user:** Has been named (mirror worked), now needs to not feel alone. The human face compounds what the AI mirror started.

These can be the same person on different days.

**Narrowest wedge (untested):** The mirror moment — one sentence that lands. A user would pay for reliable, precise mirroring at 2am. The vent and Glimpses are additive, not the wedge.

---

## Constraints

- Must not compromise the "not a chatbot" positioning. The voice agent providing affirming words must feel like **witness**, not conversation.
- Glimpses must not create parasocial attachment to individual creators. Team-curated, no engagement metrics visible to users.
- Voice vent sessions must be architecturally isolated from the session/mirror data model — no cross-contamination.
- Emotional matching for Glimpses depends on the existing emotional_metadata pipeline (primaryEmotion, granularLabel, intensity).
- ElevenLabs is already in the Phase 2 tech stack; using it for affirming words is feasible.

---

## Premises

1. **Articulation is the core problem** — people lack language. The mirror validates this. CAVEAT (from second opinion): the vent-only experience may be equally or more retentive than the mirror. This is an open test, not a settled premise. **Add to roadmap: measure 30-day retention, vent-only users vs. mirror users.**

2. **"Not a chatbot" requires UX walls, not just philosophy.** Users arrive expecting a chatbot. The voice agent and dual-entry design must encode this distinction into the interaction, not just the copy.

3. **Ephemeral relief may drive retention more than history.** The burn-the-paper behavior and the "only when it's too much" cadence suggest weekly episodic use, not daily habit. History is premium differentiation — not the core retention hook.

4. **$9.99/month freemium is the working hypothesis, untested.** Willingness to pay has not been tested against actual users.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Adjacent Sessions
Voice Vent as 4th path post-mirror. Glimpses inside Peer Reflections path.
- Effort: S. Ships fast. Wrong sequencing — vent after naming defeats the purpose for release-seeking users.

### Approach B: Dual Entry + Contextual Glimpse (chosen)
Two parallel entry points at Idle. Glimpses at session end matched to emotional fingerprint.
- Effort: M. Correct user sequencing. Glimpses hit at peak resonance.

### Approach C: The Three Flames
Full restructure — three equal modes from Idle (Name It, Let It Out, You're Not Alone).
- Effort: XL. Most complete vision, most dangerous to current retention flow. 18-month horizon.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Dual Entry + Contextual Glimpse**

### Voice Vent Box

**Entry point:** At Idle state, alongside "What's here right now?" — a secondary option: "Just need to let it out →"

**Interaction design:**
1. User taps "Let it out"
2. Screen transitions to a minimal recording interface — no text, no texture words. Just a soft waveform visualizer and a mic button.
3. User records. ElevenLabs agent detects natural pauses and responds with affirming presence — not reflection, not analysis, not advice. Phrases like: "I'm here." "That's real." "You're not carrying this alone." Calibrated to match energy (raw intensity gets quiet presence; low affect gets warmth).
4. When user stops, a beat of silence. Then: **"Gone."** Visual — the waveform fades, visually dissolves.
5. No session end screen. No path selection. No contribution prompt. Close to Idle.

**Architecture — zero-write by design:**
- Vent sessions are NOT `sessions` table entries. No Convex insert of any kind. Zero server writes.
- Audio captured via `expo-av` on device. Never uploaded. Streamed to ElevenLabs agent via client-side SDK only.
- ElevenLabs processes audio and returns affirming audio response — no transcript, no storage, no metadata persisted.
- "Gone." is architecturally real, not just UX theater. The privacy statement shown once on first vent: "This session exists only on your device while you speak. Nothing is stored."
- **Fallback (if ElevenLabs Conversational AI SDK requires bare workflow):** Record via `expo-av`, detect pause via silence threshold, play a pre-recorded affirming audio clip selected by basic amplitude/energy analysis client-side. No network call needed. Test this path first before committing to the agent SDK.

**ElevenLabs agent configuration (system prompt constraints):**
- System prompt must include: "You NEVER ask questions. You NEVER offer advice. You NEVER reflect back what was said. You only provide brief affirming presence: 'I hear you.' 'That's real.' 'You're not carrying this alone.' Match energy: louder/faster speech → quieter presence. Softer speech → warmer presence. Maximum 8 words per response."
- Illustrative configuration (verify against ElevenLabs Conversational AI SDK docs before implementing — field names not confirmed): `interrupt_on_pause: false`, `end_call_on_silence_timeout: 5` (seconds), `turn_detection: false`.

**Idle UX with two entry points:**
- Primary: "What's here right now?" — full-width, existing prompt, existing texture words below
- Secondary: A quieter, smaller prompt beneath — approximately 60% opacity, smaller text: "Just need to let it out →"
- No equal weight. The mirror flow is the default. The vent is a secondary door for users who know they want it.
- On first launch, a one-time tooltip: "This path is voice-only and disappears when you're done."

**Error states:**
- Mic permission denied: "This path needs your microphone. Tap here to open settings." Skip option returns to Idle.
- ElevenLabs connection failure (or fallback mode active): silently switch to pre-recorded affirming audio. User experience unchanged. No error shown.
- Mic hardware failure: "Something's off with your microphone." Soft dismiss to Idle.

**The design wall against chatbot confusion:**
- No text input. No back-and-forth. The agent never asks a question (enforced by system prompt above).
- The waveform is the UI — user talks, waves respond, agent presence is audio-only.
- Session ends when user stops, not when agent "finishes responding."

### Glimpses

**Entry point:** Session end screen, after mirror flow completion (any path — Solo, Peers, or Exit).

**Placement:** Below the "Heard." or "You showed up for yourself today." message — a soft transition: *"Someone who felt something close to this."* → video card appears.

**Matching logic:**
- Pull `primaryEmotion` (string) + `intensityScore` (0.0–1.0) from `emotional_metadata` for this session. These fields exist on the schema as `primaryEmotion` and `primaryEmotionConfidence` (used as a proxy for intensity where no raw intensity field exists — verify field names against current schema before implementing).
- Intensity tiers: Low = 0.0–0.39, Mid = 0.40–0.74, High = 0.75–1.0. "± 1 tier" means: if session is Mid, match Glimpses with `intensityRange` overlapping Low–High.
- Query Glimpses library: `emotionTags` contains `primaryEmotion` AND `intensityRange` overlaps the ± 1 tier window AND `glimpse_views` does NOT contain this profileId + glimpseId in last 30 days.
- If no match: fallback to broadly resonant entries tagged `["grief", "heaviness", "numbness", "weight"]` — these should always have coverage.
- **No-Glimpse empty state:** If no videos exist at all (pre-launch or content gaps), the session-end screen renders normally without the Glimpse prompt. The "Someone who felt something close to this." line does NOT appear. No broken card, no placeholder. Silent omission.
- `contributorAlias` is for internal admin use only (tracking which contributor's video). Never displayed to users.

**Video design:**
- 60-90 seconds. Vertical. Face on camera. Direct address.
- Contributor says: what they felt (in their own words), one thing that helped them understand it, "I'm still here."
- No music. No B-roll. No production polish beyond basic lighting. Realness is the asset.
- Contributors are team-sourced (not UGC). Signed releases. No engagement counts visible to users.
- Rotation: user never sees the same Glimpse twice in 30 days.
- Skip option always visible — never forced.

**Schema additions needed:**
```
glimpses table:
  _id, videoHostingId: string, duration: number,  // ID from Bunny.net or Mux
  emotionTags: v.array(v.string()),
  intensityMin: v.number(), intensityMax: v.number(),
  contributorAlias: string,  // internal admin use only, never shown to users
  isActive: boolean, _creationTime
  index: by_isActive (["isActive"])  // for fetching active pool; emotion filtering
                                     // is in-memory at 30–50 video scale

glimpse_views table:
  profileId: v.id("emotional_profiles"), glimpseId: v.id("glimpses"), viewedAt: v.number()
  index: by_profileId_and_glimpseId (["profileId", "glimpseId"])
  index: by_profileId (["profileId"])  // for "not seen in 30 days" query
```
Note: At 30–50 videos, emotion/intensity filtering can be done in-memory after fetching the active pool. No search index needed until the library scales past ~500 entries.

**Privacy:** Contributors filmed knowingly and consented. No user emotional data ever associated with a Glimpse entry. The matching is read-only — emotion tag lookup only.

## Open Questions

1. **Vent retention test (critical):** Does the ephemeral vent create a standalone usage habit? 30-day retention, vent-only cohort vs. mirror cohort. This is the most important experiment in the next 6 months.
2. **Glimpses supply side:** How many Glimpses needed at launch to feel alive? Estimate: 30-50 videos covering the 8-10 core emotion clusters at 2-3 intensity levels. That's a production sprint before launch.
3. **ElevenLabs agent latency on mobile:** Response latency for the affirming words needs to feel natural, not laggy. Test on actual devices before committing to this interaction model.
4. **"Gone." verifiability:** Users may not trust the UI. Consider a transparent privacy statement shown once: "Voice sessions are processed locally and never written to our servers. We cannot access them." Or a visible server-side confirmation indicator.
5. **Vent + Glimpse combination:** Does a user who just vented want to see a Glimpse? The current design gates Glimpses behind the mirror flow only. Worth testing whether Glimpses at vent-session-end have similar impact.
6. **Two-entry Idle UX:** How do you present two entry points without creating decision paralysis? The emotionally burdened user doesn't want to choose a mode. Consider: default to mirror flow, secondary option is quieter/smaller — not a full 50/50 split.

## Success Criteria

- **Voice vent:** ≥40% of vent-session users return within 7 days without push notification
- **Glimpses:** ≥60% of users who see a Glimpse watch >80% of it (skip rate <40%)
- **Vent retention test:** Sufficient cohort data within 60 days of launch to compare vent-only vs. mirror retention
- **No chatbot confusion:** Zero user reports of "I thought I could talk to the AI" after vent box UX is live (qualitative)

## Distribution Plan

Existing deployment pipeline covers this (EAS + TestFlight). No new distribution channel needed.

**Glimpses video hosting:** Use Bunny.net Stream or Mux (both cost-effective, HLS-native, no bandwidth spike risk) over direct Convex storage. Videos are read-heavy, not write-heavy. Between the two: Bunny.net is cheaper at low volume; Mux has better analytics and a React Native SDK. Pick based on budget — either works for the video volume described. Videos should not be public — use signed URL generation (both services support this) called from a Convex action at view time. `videoHostingId` in the schema stores the video ID from whichever service is chosen. The serving layer generates a time-limited signed URL on demand and returns it to the client.

## The Assignment

Before you write one line of code for Voice Vent or Glimpses:

**Record 3 Glimpses this week.** Find 3 people. Get them on camera. 60-90 seconds. Ask them to describe a time they felt something they couldn't name — what it was, what helped, and that they're still here. Don't produce it. Just capture it.

Then show those 3 videos to 5 users after their next Xolace session. Watch their faces. That reaction — or lack of it — tells you more than any architecture decision.
